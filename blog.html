A PAW-ESOME RESCUE

"Only if we understand can we care. Only if we care will we help. Only if we help shall they be saved."
-Jane Goodall

    It was another ordinary rainy Spetember day here in Malolos. Unfortunaely for us citizens, a typhoon was reported to pass through the city for the next few days earlier this week. Not long after the report came a sudden heavy rainfall that soon swept the streets with floods in record time. Dark clouds reigned over my humble abode. I prefer a much more sunny background when I wake up all groggily. But nevertheless, I always loved waking up in a middle of an actual storm. It’s cold which means I could just stay in bed, read a book, drink hot chocolate and even just sleep...if only I was required to come to work and take care of the animals. Furthermore, what I didn’t expect is that it will be the day where an unforeseen angel will come in to my life and brigthens my days instantly.

     It was already late and so I quickly grabbed my belongings and rushed out of my apartment, after having breakfast and a warm bath of course. I was already late for work any way but somehow I was still irritated by how time pass by the jeep I rode. There is a recent road construction coming all the way from Longos to Alido and so, it was accompanied by heavy traffic plus the heavy rain. It was greatly annoying and irritating. Everyone is trying to get past each other. It was only a 10-15 minutes walk left to the Bulcan State University so I might as well start walking ahead of traffic.

    Halfway through I began hearing small whimpers on the side of the road. I stopped dead in my tracks. I was suddenly came face to face to a stray skinny dog desperately looking for food in the trash and was severely wounded. As the professional veterinarian I was, I managed to keep my bearings and quickly spawned into action. I persuaded the dog to come with me. She was hesitant at first but she was desperate and so she accepted my offer. She looked so pitiful when I took her inside the institution then into our department. Seeing stray animals in the streets of Malolos is not a new thing for its citizens. In fact, it is very common to see dogs near trash bins, cats in the sidewalks, and sometimes you will realize they are staring right at you hoping for some food to eat. Yes, the only thing all these animals wish for is love and care. They just want a home that will make them feel special and live a comfortable life.

Here are her symptoms when I first saw her:

  1. Dozens of fleas and parasites clung to her body as they suck all the little blood she has left in her system
  2. There were numerous bald spots in her fur
  3. She smelled awful due to her scavenging for food in trashbins
  4. Blood soaked both her head and feet and one of them was fractured and broken
  5. Her body almost looked like being decayed already

    When I brought her in, the whole team were screaming in fright. We haven’t seen such nasty situation of an animal for months now. But of course, it was nothing new to our agency. We have to deal with it somehow and it was recommended that we do it immediately. Most of the time it were animals with fleas and stuffs and sometimes, it were worst. Sometimes they were bloody, abused and most scaringly, “disabled”. Those times shook me down to nausea every time. 

    We took her inside the shelter with warmth and love, gave her a warm bath filled with antibacterial solution and thoroughly washed the fleas away, fed her with medicine that would eventually help her regrow her fur, and bandage her broken leg. The team and I didn't know what else to do and so we just kept doing the same procedure because we don't know if the dog would be traumatic to her past or at least have trust issues.    

    We decided that it was best for her to be with someone almost the entire day every day and that someone has to be me. Besides, I would gladly enjoy the company at my apartment. What could go wrong? Apparently, the dog has a strong spirit within her as she was energetic around the apartment. It's been weeks since the incident and her rescue and she a a new set of fur and an amazing set of personality as well as grew very fond of me and vice versa. I named her Allie after a video game character because somehow I knew this would be the start of an extraordinary adventure like in the game.

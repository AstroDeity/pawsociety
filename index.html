ONE PET, ONE OWNER

Their paws are in our hands

Have you ever notice the number of stray animals you often come across with on your way to work? How about the times you saw them roaming around the canteen? Do they wait for you to throw your leftovers outside your home? Practically anywhere you go, there seems to be no end to our encounters with them. That is where we come in. Greetings! We, the members of Paw Society, are determine to aid, heal, and save these animals from these unfortunate situation as ell as raise awareness of their existence and find a suitable and loving home within you.

OUR CAUSE

Paw Society is a non-profit organization that works
through public education, research, rescue, legislation,  
special events and, when needed, direct action in order
to achieve our goals and aspirations.

Reduce the Population of Stray Animals

Create a Bond Between Man and Animal

Promote the Awareness of Animal Protection

prevent accidents invloving  stray animals

A PAW-ESOME RESCUE

                 by Patrick Gonzales

     Seeing stray animals in the streets of Malolos is
not a new thing for its citizens. In fact, it is very
common to see dogs near trash bins, cats in the
sidewalks, and sometimes you will realize they are
staring right at you hoping for some food to eat.
Yes, the only thing all these animals wish for is love
and care. They just want a home that will make
them feel special and live a comfortable life.  
< . . . >
What I didn’t expect is that it will be the day
where an unforeseen angel will come in
to my life and brigthens my days
instantly.
